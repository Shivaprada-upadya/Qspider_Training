ANDAMAN NICOBAR ISLAND


The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are composed of a chain of 572 islands, of which only 38 are inhabited. These islands are geographically divided into two main groups: the Andaman Islands in the north and the Nicobar Islands in the south, separated by the Ten Degree Channel. The islands are characterized by a diverse topography, with sedimentary rocks like sandstone, limestone, and shale forming the terrain.
