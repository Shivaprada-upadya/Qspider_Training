ANDAMAN NICOBAR ISLAND


The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are composed of a chain of 572 islands, of which only 38 are inhabited. These islands are geographically divided into two main groups: the Andaman Islands in the north and the Nicobar Islands in the south, separated by the Ten Degree Channel.

KASHMIR

Kashmir refers to a region in the northwestern part of the Indian subcontinent, known for its diverse geography, culture, and history. It is a region of contention between India, Pakistan, and China. Administratively, the region is divided into the Indian union territory of Jammu and Kashmir, the union territory of Ladakh, and the Pakistani-administered territories of Azad Kashmir and Gilgit-Baltistan.

LASKSHDWEEP

Lakshadweep is a captivating archipelago and India's smallest Union Territory, comprised of 36 islands, 12 atolls, three reefs, and five submerged banks. Known for its stunning coral reefs, white sandy beaches, and vibrant lagoons, Lakshadweep offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Only ten of the islands are inhabited, with Kavaratti serving as the capital.
